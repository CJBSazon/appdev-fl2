FIELD PREPARATION & PLANTING

Field preparation and planting are critical stages in rice cultivation, laying the foundation for successful crop establishment and optimal yield. Field preparation involves clearing the land of previous crop residues, weeds, and debris to create a clean and level seedbed for rice cultivation. Farmers then plow or till the soil to improve its structure and facilitate root penetration, ensuring adequate aeration and drainage. Proper leveling of the field helps to achieve uniform water distribution during the flooding or irrigation phase, which is crucial for rice growth. Additionally, farmers may apply organic amendments such as compost or manure to enrich the soil with essential nutrients and improve fertility. Once the field is prepared, rice seeds are either directly broadcasted into the prepared soil or first germinated in nurseries before being transplanted into flooded paddies. This methodical approach to field preparation and planting sets the stage for healthy crop growth and high yields in rice farming.
